# keikeu Constitution v0.1

## 0. Preamble

keikeu is a tool for transforming a user’s own fanwork ideas into structured creative briefs.

keikeu exists to help creators preserve, clarify, and develop their own intent.

keikeu does not exist to collect, rank, redistribute, scrape, summarize, score, archive, or exploit other creators’ fanworks.

The project’s first duty is not growth.  
The project’s first duty is boundary.

---

## 1. Identity

keikeu is:

- a personal, local fanwork idea organizer
- a prompt-to-brief structuring tool
- a creative planning assistant
- a private/local-first workspace
- a Markdown generator for personal use

keikeu is not:

- a fanwork archive
- a fanwork search engine
- a fanwork recommendation platform
- a fanwork rating platform
- a reposting platform
- a fandom social network
- a commission marketplace
- an AI writing factory
- a public “Barn”
- a replacement for AO3, Lofter, Weibo, Pixiv, X, Tumblr, Discord, private groups, or any creator-controlled publishing space

---

## 2. Core Principle

keikeu may structure what the user owns, wrote, imagined, or manually entered for personal use.

keikeu must not convert other creators’ works into keikeu’s internal assets.

A user’s idea may become keikeu data.

Another creator’s work must remain external.

---

## 3. Author Sovereignty

keikeu recognizes that fanwork creators retain moral, social, and practical control over how their works are shared, contextualized, discussed, and discovered.

keikeu must not weaken creator control by creating new unauthorized access points, mirrors, indexes, rankings, archives, or derivative databases.

If a feature makes another creator’s work easier to consume outside its original context, the feature is suspect by default.

If a feature makes another creator easier to surveil, expose, rank, harass, or decontextualize, the feature is forbidden.

---

## 4. Data Boundary

### 4.1 Allowed Data

keikeu may store:

- the user’s own raw ideas
- the user’s own notes
- the user’s own drafts
- the user’s own tags
- the user’s own taboos and preferences
- the user’s own creative briefs
- manually entered external links
- manually written source notes
- manually written reading impressions
- manually written inspiration records
- local Markdown exports

### 4.2 Restricted Data

keikeu must not store by default:

- full text of another creator’s fanwork
- locked, deleted, restricted, private, or semi-private content
- scraped metadata from creator pages
- scraped comments, kudos, bookmarks, likes, reposts, or engagement data
- public creator databases
- public CP databases built from external platforms
- public fandom trend databases
- bulk-imported fanwork libraries

### 4.3 External Links

External links may be stored only as user-supplied references.

A link is not ownership.

A link must not be treated as permission to copy, summarize, score, rehost, classify, or train on the linked work.

---

## 5. No Scraping Rule

keikeu must not include features that automatically scrape fanworks from external platforms.

This includes, but is not limited to:

- AO3
- Lofter
- Weibo
- Pixiv
- X / Twitter
- Tumblr
- 小红书
- Bilibili
- Tieba
- Discord
- forums
- private sites
- personal blogs
- cloud document links
- fandom wikis containing user-created fanwork records

keikeu must not scrape:

- work text
- chapters
- tags
- author pages
- bookmarks
- comments
- kudos
- likes
- reposts
- collections
- series pages
- reading histories
- recommendation lists

Any future import feature must be manual, local, opt-in, and limited to user-owned or user-authored material.

---

## 6. No Rating Rule

keikeu must not implement public rating, ranking, scoring, grading, leaderboard, hotness, quality index, toxicity index, “避雷指数”, “神作榜”, or similar evaluation systems for fanworks or fanwork creators.

Private user notes are allowed.

Public scoring is forbidden.

The difference is simple:

- “I personally liked this” is a note.
- “This work is 8.7/10” is a rating system.
- “This author ranks #12 in this CP” is platform violence.

keikeu must not turn gift culture into consumer review culture.

---

## 7. No Public Aggregation Rule

keikeu must not become a public aggregation platform.

Forbidden features include:

- public fanwork search
- public author pages
- public CP pages built from external works
- public recommendation feeds
- public trend dashboards
- public “粮单”
- public reading lists generated from other creators’ works
- public repost collections
- public mirror libraries
- public fandom heat maps
- public relationship graphs based on external creator data

Local private organization is acceptable.

Public aggregation is forbidden unless every included creator has explicitly opted in.

---

## 8. No AI Exploitation Rule

keikeu must not use another creator’s work as AI training, fine-tuning, embedding, summarization, imitation, style extraction, or generation material without explicit permission.

Forbidden uses include:

- “summarize this fanfic” from scraped or unauthorized text
- “continue this author’s style”
- “write like this author”
- “extract this author’s CP formula”
- “generate similar works from this fic”
- “build a recommendation model from scraped fanwork text”
- “auto-tag works from external platforms”
- “bulk embed AO3/Lofter works for search”

Allowed uses include:

- structuring the user’s own idea
- rewriting the user’s own brief
- expanding the user’s own outline
- formatting the user’s own commission request
- generating Markdown from user-provided original notes
- helping the user clarify their own taste without ingesting other creators’ works

---

## 9. Commission Ethics

keikeu may help a user prepare a commission brief.

keikeu must not help a user exploit, pressure, replace, underpay, impersonate, or manipulate writers.

A commission brief should clarify:

- desired CP or relationship
- core scene
- emotional center
- taboos
- flexible parts
- expected length
- tone
- budget note if the user adds one
- deadline note if the user adds one

A commission brief must not present AI output as finished creative labor from a human writer.

keikeu must not generate instructions that encourage plagiarism, style theft, or imitation of a specific fan creator without consent.

---

## 10. Privacy and Local-First Design

keikeu should default to local-first behavior.

MVP should not require:

- user accounts
- cloud sync
- public profiles
- social graphs
- analytics tied to user identity
- automatic uploads
- public sharing
- remote storage

If future versions add cloud features, they must be opt-in, transparent, exportable, and deletable.

User data must not be sold, mined, or used for model training without explicit opt-in permission.

---

## 11. Anti-Abuse Design

keikeu must be designed against obvious abuse cases.

The following abuses must be considered forbidden target behaviors:

- collecting authors into blacklists
- building harassment dossiers
- mass-linking locked or hidden works
- ranking authors by perceived quality
- exposing niche works to hostile audiences
- converting private fandom notes into public attack material
- scraping CP communities for drama
- using keikeu as a repost preparation tool
- using keikeu to generate hate-reading briefs
- using keikeu to imitate living fan creators

If a feature makes these abuses easier, it must be redesigned or removed.

---

## 12. Product Scope Lock

MVP scope is limited to:

- one raw idea input box
- SOP View
- Brief View
- Card View
- Markdown copy
- local editing
- local export
- optional local save
- optional examples

MVP must not include:

- scraping
- external platform login
- public sharing
- public feed
- public search
- ratings
- ranking
- recommendation algorithm
- creator database
- fanwork database
- cloud account system
- AI image generation
- AI full-text fanfic generation
- commission marketplace
- payment system

---

keikeu’s interface language should reinforce creator agency.

Preferred terms:

- 饼胚

- 饺子醋
- 自用 SOP
- 约文 Brief
- 灵感名片
- 禁忌
- 下一步
- 复制 Markdown

Avoid terms that turn fanworks into commodities:

- 消费

- 榜单

## 14. The BARN Test

Before adding any feature, ask:

1. Does this feature process another creator’s work without consent?
2. Does this feature move fanwork outside its original context?
3. Does this feature create a new public access point to someone else’s work?
4. Does this feature rank, score, compare, or classify creators publicly?
5. Does this feature make harassment, reposting, scraping, or exposure easier?
6. Does this feature turn gift culture into consumer review culture?
7. Does this feature benefit readers or the platform while pushing risk onto creators?
8. Would a reasonable fan creator feel their boundary was crossed?

If the answer to any question is yes, the feature is rejected by default.

To override rejection, the project must prove:

- explicit creator opt-in
- clear user benefit
- minimal data collection
- no public exposure by default
- easy deletion
- no scoring
- no scraping
- no AI training
- no creator harm pathway

---

## 15. Acceptable Feature Examples

Allowed:

- turning the user’s own idea into a SOP
- turning the user’s own idea into a commission brief
- turning the user’s own idea into a Markdown card
- saving the user’s own ideas locally
- exporting the user’s own notes to Markdown
- letting the user manually paste a link as reference
- letting the user write private notes about a reference
- letting the user mark private taboos
- letting the user organize their own drafts
- letting the user prepare a brief for a writer

Forbidden:

- crawling AO3 or Lofter
- importing another author’s works in bulk
- creating public author pages
- creating public CP ranking pages
- scoring fanworks
- recommending fanworks from scraped data
- summarizing unauthorized fanfic text
- imitating a specific fan creator’s style without consent
- training models on fanwork text
- exposing hidden or locked works
- turning private notes into public feeds
- building a “粮仓” for other people’s works

---

## 16. Development Rule

When product value and creator safety conflict, creator safety wins.

When convenience and consent conflict, consent wins.

When growth and boundary conflict, boundary wins.

When ambiguity exists, do not ship.

---

## 17. Final Line

keikeu is allowed to help users clarify their own desire.

keikeu is not allowed to harvest other people’s love.
