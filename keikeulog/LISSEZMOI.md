# keikeu

**keikeu** is a fanwork idea-to-brief tool.

It turns raw shipping ideas, scene cravings, and fandom rambling into structured creative briefs.

**keikeu 是一个同人创作饼胚整理工具**，把 CP 口嗨、名场面执念和零散灵感转换成结构化创作说明。

> 把“我想看这个”变成“这个该怎么做”。

---

## What is keikeu?

keikeu is not a general AI writing tool.

It does not aim to replace writers, artists, or fandom creators. Its first goal is smaller and sharper:

> Turn emotional, fragmented fanwork ideas into usable creative SOPs.

In plain terms:

```text
口嗨 → 饼胚 SOP
```

A user may begin with only a vague craving, a CP scene, a role dynamic, a painful image, or a half-joking fandom ramble. keikeu helps reshape that raw material into a structured brief that can be used for self-writing, commissioning, outlining, archiving, or further development.

---

## Product Positioning

keikeu is a **fanwork idea-to-brief tool**.

It helps users convert:

- CP rambling
- scene cravings
- character relationship ideas
- fandom “what if” scenarios
- unfinished plot fragments
- commission intentions
- emotional creative impulses

into:

- structured creative briefs
- writing SOPs
- commission-ready descriptions
- outline seeds
- reusable idea cards

The product is not built around project management. It is built around **creative translation**:

> translating “I know the vibe” into “someone else can understand and execute this.”

---

## Target Customers

### Primary Customer: Rambling-Type Fanwork Creators

The primary users are fanwork creators who have strong CP, character, or plot ideas, but struggle to express them in a structured way.

They are not short on inspiration.

They are stuck because their inspiration is too emotional to deliver.

Typical state:

```text
脑子里有名场面。
嘴上能嗨半小时。
真要写成约稿说明 / 大纲 / 开头，就卡死。
```

Typical user thoughts:

> 我知道我想嗑什么，但我说不清楚。  
> 我有一堆画面，但串不成故事。  
> 我想约稿，但不知道怎么写需求。  
> 我怕 AI / 写手 / 画师误解我的 CP 味。  
> 我不想一上来就写正文，只想先把饼摆出来。

This is keikeu's core customer:

> **口嗨型同人创作者。**

---

### Secondary Customer: Commission Initiators

The secondary users are people who want to commission fanwork but cannot clearly describe what they want.

They may want to commission:

- fanfiction
- illustration
- comics
- video edits
- setting documents
- character relationship concepts

Their core need is:

> 把脑内 CP 妄想转成给创作者看的需求单。

For this group, keikeu functions as a **commission brief translator**.

It reduces the gap between:

```text
我脑子里有东西
```

and:

```text
写手 / 画师 / 剪辑师能看懂我要什么
```

---

### Tertiary Customer: Self-Entertainment Idea Archivists

The tertiary users are fandom participants who may not immediately produce finished work.

They simply want to store their ideas beautifully and clearly.

Their state is:

```text
我今天嗑到了。
我先存一下。
以后可能写。
也可能永远不写。
但我要它看起来像个漂亮橱窗。
```

This group has long-term retention value, but it should not dominate the first version of the product.

If keikeu designs too much around collection and archiving too early, it risks becoming a low-energy Notion template instead of a sharp creative tool.

---

## Core Job-to-be-Done

When I have a fandom craving, help me turn it into a usable creative brief.

More explicitly:

> 当我有一段很想嗑 / 很想写 / 很想约的灵感时，帮我把它整理成别人能理解、我自己也能继续推进的创作 SOP。

---

## Core Input and Output

### Input

A raw idea, also called a **饼胚**.

Examples:

```text
A 和 B 曾是一对 CP，某一日，B 变成了一只小虫。
```

```text
我想看 A 发现 B 变成虫后，第一反应不是害怕，而是把它藏进袖口。
```

```text
一个很冷的 AU：昔日宿敌在末日后被迫共享一具身体，但只有夜里能对话。
```

The input can be messy. That is the point.

It may be:

- a sentence
- a paragraph
- a scene craving
- a CP fantasy
- a plot fragment
- a commission idea
- a casual fandom ramble

---

### Output

A structured creative SOP.

Minimum output sections:

```text
1. 原始灵感 I
2. Tags
3. 饺子醋
4. Summary
5. 饺子
6. 成品形态
7. 禁忌
8. 下一步
```

---

## Output Structure

### 1. 原始灵感 I

Preserve the user's original idea.

Do not over-polish it. Do not erase its raw flavor.

This section keeps the original creative impulse intact.

---

### 2. Tags

Extract the core elements of the idea.

Possible tag categories:

- CP / relationship
- characters
- AU
- genre
- emotional tone
- kink / moe point
- narrative device
- conflict type
- ending tendency
- commission type

---

### 3. 饺子醋

The non-negotiable scene, image, line, action, or relationship beat.

This is the part that must remain.

Do not treat it as a random highlight. It is the anchor of the whole brief.

Example:

```text
A 发现 B 变成虫后，第一反应不是害怕，而是把它藏进袖口。
```

The job of keikeu is to protect this core craving, then build the story around it.

---

### 4. Summary

A short explanation of what the work is probably about.

This should usually be 3-6 sentences.

It should clarify:

- who the story follows
- what changes
- what conflict appears
- what emotional direction the work takes
- why the central craving matters

---

### 5. 饺子

The supporting material needed to make the 饺子醋 work.

This may include:

- setup
- character motivation
- relationship context
- scene order
- emotional buildup
- conflict escalation
- transition logic
- ending direction

In short:

> 饺子醋是必须吃到的味道，饺子是让它能被端上桌的结构。

---

### 6. 成品形态

Suggest what form the idea should become.

Possible forms:

- short fic
- medium-length fic
- long fic
- outline
- commission brief
- illustration prompt
- comic storyboard
- video script
- setting sheet
- character relationship sheet

The MVP should prioritize text-based forms first:

- self-writing brief
- fanfiction commission brief
- outline draft
- Markdown archive card

---

### 7. 禁忌

Optional but important restrictions.

This section defines what must not happen.

Examples:

- do not break a specific character interpretation
- do not change the CP dynamic
- do not add unrelated third-party romance
- do not make the tone comedic
- do not erase the tragedy
- do not write explicit content
- do not use a disliked trope
- avoid specific triggers or squicks

This section protects the user's taste.

For fanwork, taste protection is not decoration. It is product core.

---

### 8. 下一步

Give a concrete next action.

Possible next steps:

- directly start writing
- expand into a full outline
- turn into a commission brief
- create a scene list
- write character notes
- make an illustration brief
- save as an idea card
- continue brainstorming

The output is successful only if the user can move.

---

## MVP Scope

The first version of keikeu should do only three things:

1. Accept a raw fanwork idea.
2. Generate a structured creative SOP.
3. Let the user copy the result as Markdown.

That is enough.

Do not expand too early.

---

## Must-Have Features

- Input box for raw idea / 饼胚
- Generate button
- Structured SOP output
- Markdown formatting
- Copy Markdown button
- Editable output fields
- Clear section labels
- Beginner-readable interface

---

## Not in MVP

keikeu should not include these in the first version:

- account system
- community publishing
- social feed
- direct marketplace
- payment
- full database
- multi-user collaboration
- complex project management
- image generation
- full fanfiction generation
- copyright review system
- platform moderation system

These may be future features, but adding them too early will damage the core product.

The first version must stay sharp:

```text
饼胚 → SOP
```

---

## Product Principle

The tool should not steal the user's creative agency.

It should help the user clarify, display, and hand off an idea.

The key product principle:

> Do not replace the craving. Structure it.

---

## Success Criteria

keikeu succeeds when the user can do at least one of the following after reading the output:

- start writing
- expand the outline
- commission a creator
- save the idea cleanly
- explain the idea to someone else
- identify what must not be changed

It fails if the output is pretty but unusable.

It also fails if it turns every idea into generic AI-flavored mush.

---

## Development Direction

The first technical version should be simple:

```text
local single-page app
raw input
mock / rule-based output first
Markdown export
AI integration later
```

The first build should validate the product logic before adding model complexity.

Do not start with backend, login, database, or marketplace features.

Start with the smallest useful loop:

```text
输入一段口嗨 → 输出一份能用的饼胚 SOP
```
